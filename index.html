Austerity Measures in the Public Sector in Turkey

Turkey's new austerity measures aim to address the economic crisis by cutting investments and service procurement, potentially reforming taxes to save 1 trillion lira. Announced by Finance Minister Mehmet Şimşek and Vice President Cevdet Yılmaz, these measures seek to reduce the budget deficit. Critiques emphasize equitable burden distribution and the symbolic importance of leadership in implementing these changes. The political implications and necessity of these measures are also highlighted.


This report analyzes social media discussions about Turkey's new austerity measures, drawing insights from posts and comments on Instagram and Facebook. The data was collected using hashtags related to public sector austerity, including #kamudatasarruf, #memurhakkınıalamıyor, #tasarruftedbirleri, and #verimlilikpaketi. The analysis focuses on sentiment, key phrases, and co-occurring hashtags to understand public perception and provide actionable recommendations for policymakers.

About the dataset

The collected dataset consists of posts and comments from Instagram and Facebook related to four hashtags: '#publicsavings', '#publicemployeesrights', '#savingmeasures', and '#efficiencypackage'. It includes details such as post text, sentiment, intent, language, and engagement metrics. The main dataset captures a variety of public opinions, while the hashtags dataset connects these opinions to specific hashtags.

What citizens think

Sentiment analysis of the posts shows a mix of positive, negative, and neutral reactions to the new austerity measures. Overall, there is a noticeable trend of concern and dissatisfaction among citizens, but some positive sentiments indicate support for the measures’ potential long-term benefits.

Compliments

  • Efforts to Reduce Expenditures: Some citizens appreciate the efforts to reduce unnecessary government expenditures.

  • Increased Efficiency: Positive comments often mention the potential for increased efficiency and productivity in the public sector.

  • Economic Stability: There is support for long-term economic stability that these measures might bring.

Complaints

  • Insufficient Support: Citizens express frustration about the lack of sufficient government support during austerity.

  • Impact on Public Services: Many posts highlight concerns about the negative impact on public services and employee rights.

  • Unfair Targeting: There is a strong sentiment that the measures are unfairly targeting lower-income groups.

What is discussed

Co-occurring hashtags reveal additional topics of interest and trending discussions:

  • #economicsustainability:Citizens discuss the broader economic impact of the measures.

  • #governmentaccountability:There is a call for greater transparency and accountability in how the measures are implemented.

  • #publicserviceefficiency: Discussions about improving efficiency in public services are prevalent and trending.

Trending key phrases in the data highlight the main themes of public discussion:

  • Public sector cuts: Frequent mentions of public austerity reflect widespread awareness and concern about government spending cuts.

  • Employee rights:Many posts focus on the rights of public sector employees amidst austerity.

  • Economic impact: Citizens are concerned about the broader economic impact of these measures.
These key phrases underscore the need for policymakers to address public concerns transparently and ensure that austerity measures are implemented fairly and effectively. ​

Authorities Criticized

Analysis of the data reveals criticism of key authorities and institutions involved in implementing austerity measures:

  • Ministry of Treasury and Finance Criticized for harsh economic measures and lack of effective communication.

  • President Seen as out of touch with public needs and pushing harmful policies.

  • AKP (Justice and Development Party): Criticized for overall economic policies and implementation of austerity measures.

  • Government Units and Officials: Viewed as responsible for negative impacts on public services and lack of fairness.

  • Unions and Bureaus: Highlight the organized resistance and demand for fair treatment and transparency.
These key phrases underscore the need for policymakers to address public concerns transparently and ensure that austerity measures are implemented fairly and effectively. ​

To take an action

Enhance Transparency and Communication

  • Clear Information: Authorities should provide clear and detailed information about the necessity of the austerity measures and the expected outcomes.

  • Build Trust: This can help build public trust and reduce misinformation.

Implement Targeted Support

  • Support Programs: Develop targeted support programs for the most affected groups, particularly lower-income citizens, to mitigate the negative impacts of the austerity measures.

  • Social Services: This can include financial aid, job training, and other social services.

Engage with Public Sector Employees

  • Address Concerns: Increase engagement with public sector employees by addressing their concerns and improving morale during this transition period.

  • Maintain Productivity: Regular consultations, feedback mechanisms, and support initiatives can help maintain productivity and ensure smoother implementation of the measures.
